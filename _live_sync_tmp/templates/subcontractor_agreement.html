1. Relationship of the Parties

The parties intend to create an independent contractor relationship. The Subcontractor is not being engaged as a W-2 employee, and the Subcontractor remains responsible for the Subcontractor's own taxes, licensing, insurance, staffing, tools, training, and business obligations, except where applicable law or a separate signed agreement requires something else.

This agreement documents the parties' intent and onboarding record. It does not override IRS, Department of Labor, OSHA, state workers compensation, or court classification rules if the actual working relationship is different from the facts recorded here.

6. Safety and Jobsite Duties

The Subcontractor is responsible for safe work practices, training, supervision of its own helpers, required protective equipment, and compliance with OSHA and jobsite safety requirements that apply to the Subcontractor's work. Nothing in this agreement removes any safety duties that may apply to the Business as a host, controlling, creating, exposing, or correcting employer under applicable law.

7. Employee Benefits Disclosure

The Subcontractor acknowledges that this engagement is not enrollment in employee benefit programs, paid employee leave, payroll withholding, unemployment benefits, or W-2 employee payroll unless applicable law or a separate written plan requires otherwise.

8. Term and Termination
